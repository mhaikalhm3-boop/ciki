Modeling Harumanis Canopy Expansion via Calculus Derivatives

In precision fruit farming, mapping static tree sizes is only half the battle. By calculating the mathematical derivative $f'(x)$, we can extract the exact, instantaneous Growth Velocity at any given month to map optimal fertilizer delivery and field canopy spacing.

The Bioscience Scenario

Tracking individual tree canopy radius development of the Perlis Harumanis mango across a standard 12-month vegetative cycle. Identifying developmental speeds allows agrotech specialists to accurately deliver targeted potassium and nitrogen loads.

Data Baseline Reference: Derived using vegetative indices from the Department of Agriculture (DOA) Malaysia Perlis: 'Manual Teknologi Penanaman Mangga Harumanis' and the MARDI Crop Growth Repository.

The Mathematical Model

Canopy Radius Function, $f(x)$
f(x) = -0.0012x³ + 0.024x² + 0.05x + 0.35

Predicts individual tree canopy radius in meters (m) at month $x$.

Growth Velocity Function, $f'(x)$
f'(x) = -0.0036x² + 0.048x + 0.05

Calculates the precise acceleration/rate of change in meters per month (m/month).
